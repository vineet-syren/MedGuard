# MedGuard Market Research

## Customer Base

MedGuard targets enterprise life-sciences content teams that need faster content delivery without weakening compliance discipline.

- Brand and omnichannel marketing teams need faster HCP content and personalization.
- Medical affairs teams need source-backed scientific narratives and claim integrity.
- MLR, regulatory, and compliance teams need fewer avoidable review issues.
- Content operations teams need reuse, metadata quality, lifecycle control, and visibility.
- Regional affiliates need local adaptation without breaking master-content lineage.
- Agency partners need structured briefs, approved modules, and faster feedback loops.

## Market Map

The market already has strong systems of record and production systems:

- Veeva Vault PromoMats: regulated content lifecycle, MLR review, claims, DAM, modular content, and distribution.
- Viseven eWizard: pharma-specific content authoring, modular content, and AI-assisted compliant content workflows.
- Adobe GenStudio: enterprise content supply chain and generative campaign production.
- Aprimo: DAM, content operations, metadata, governance, and agentic asset workflows.
- Salesforce and IQVIA: life-sciences engagement, CRM, commercial orchestration, and analytics.
- Indegene and enterprise AI providers: life-sciences commercialization services, AI workbenches, and domain-specific transformation.

## Positioning Decision

MedGuard should not be positioned as another DAM, CRM, MLR repository, or generic content generator.

MedGuard should be positioned as an agentic regulated content engineering layer. It prepares evidence-bound, metadata-ready, review-aware content packages before they enter the formal system of record.

## Product Implications

- Build around buyer outcomes: reduce MLR rework, trace every claim, make modular content reusable, accelerate global-to-local, coordinate launch readiness, and control agency production.
- Add explicit downstream modules for claims governance, compliance validation, responsible AI governance, workflow orchestration, ecosystem integration, and POC readiness.
- Keep the agent story bounded: AI plans, retrieves, assembles, pre-checks, routes, and explains; humans approve scientific, legal, regulatory, and final release decisions.
- Make integrations explicit with typed tool contracts and auditable handoffs.
- Keep RAG source binding and Pydantic schemas central to every high-risk agent output.
